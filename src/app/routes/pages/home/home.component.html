Health Track

Achieving and maintaining a healthy lifestyle can be a challenge for many people as the busy nature of many lives means they often fall into bad habits because of their ease or speed. Tracking your health can make maintaining a healthy lifestyle much easier for the growing number of people that find it difficult to balance work, life and wellbeing.

Wealth Track

There is a popular saying that “the easiest way of becoming bankrupt is by not paying attention to your expenses.” There is nothing that captures the importance of keeping track of your expenses better than that saying. Being aware of our spending habits is the best way of utilizing our money. When you know how much money you spend, it’s easy to balance your income with your spending and even save for the future.

Sleep Track

The benefits of sleep tracking are as flexible as your journey through life. It helps you make informed decisions on your health. It clears away certain doubts and you can focus on that which remains, your subjective experience on your quality of rest and what you need to reach better sleep.

Water Track

Water is essential for survival.Our bodies are made up of about 60% water, and throughout the day we lose it due to sweating, breathing and, well, going to the bathroom. That’s why it’s so important to drink plenty of water and to track the intake.

Diet Track

Measuring food intake is a crucial part of living a healthy lifestyle and can help to maintain a balanced diet and stay on top of health goals. When you track your food, you measure the amount of calories (and nutritional value) that you put into your body.

Exercise Track

Tracking the exercise progress is important because it lets you see how you are progressing in terms of reaching your previously set goals, and see where you can make changes to get you back on track.

Expenditure Track

When you track your spending, you can cut out bad spending habits, create and stick to a budget, and make sure you’re putting money toward your goals.The benefits of tracking your expenses include finding and eliminating bad spending habits, preventing overspending.
